About ACE Museum

Mission Statement

ACE Museum is dedicated to preserving and celebrating the history of tennis through its most essential piece of equipment — the racket. Our mission is to educate visitors about the evolution of racket design, the engineering behind modern frames, and the cultural impact of tennis across the globe.

Who We Serve

ACE Museum welcomes tennis players of all levels, sports enthusiasts, families, students, and anyone curious about how technology and design shape athletic performance. Whether you are a weekend player or a lifelong fan, there is something here for you.

Our Story

Founded in 2020, ACE Museum began as a private collection of vintage tennis rackets assembled by a lifelong tennis enthusiast in Pittsburgh. What started as a personal hobby grew into a public institution when the collection expanded to over 300 rackets spanning every era of the sport. Today, ACE Museum is a destination for tennis fans and design lovers from around the world.

Milestones

  • 2024: Opened the interactive Science of Spin exhibit
  • 2023: Acquired rackets used in Grand Slam finals by Federer and Serena Williams
  • 2022: Expanded to include a stringing workshop and demo area
  • 2020: ACE Museum opens its doors with 150 rackets on display

Frequently Asked Questions

Is parking available? ▼

Yes, free parking is available in the museum lot. Street parking is also available on Forbes Ave.

Can I bring my own racket? ▼

Absolutely! We encourage visitors to bring their rackets to our stringing demo area where our technicians can analyze your setup.

Is the museum accessible? ▼

Yes, ACE Museum is fully wheelchair accessible with ramps, elevators, and accessible restrooms on every floor.
